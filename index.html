My Thoughts on Web Accessablity

With how ingrained the web as became, more and more people are using it. Web Accessablity, to me, is defined by how well it can accomodate to a person and the host. A personal example. I have ASD and I have light sensitivity. 'Dark mode' accomodates for me to make my experience better. Host wise, I'd say it's important that the web can adapt to different platforms such as a phone or laptop, even a different script. Making the web more inclusive can open a lot of opportunities for people and can help economically too. According to stardust-testing.com, in their words:

"Economically, ignoring the disabled community means ignoring millions of potential customers with billions in purchasing power. In the United States alone, the purchasing power of assistive-technology Internet users is worth more than $350 billion."
